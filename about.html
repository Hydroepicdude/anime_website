About Anime

Anime is an abbreviation for the term animation. In Japan is it used to refer to all animation however it is referred to as Japanese animations outside the country. They are be computer generated or hand drawn and are characterised by their vibrant characters, fantastical themes and colourful graphics. Many westerners view anime as a Japanese product, and scholars believe that calling anime specifically Japanese may be related to the new form of Orientalism.

Anime combines the techniques of storytelling, characterisation, cinematography and graphic art. The creators of anime do not mainly focus on the animation of movement as they focus on the realism of the setting. They use camera effects such as panning zooming and angle shots. Being hand-drawn anime is separated from reality by a crucial gap of fiction that provides an ideal path for escapism that audiences can immerse themselves into with relative ease. Diverse art styles are used and character proportions and features can be quite varied, including characteristically large emotive or realistically sized eyes.

Most television anime are accompanied by Japanese pop or rock songs, often by reputed bands. They may be written with the series in mind and are also aimed at the general music market. Pop and rock songs are also sometimes used as incidental music (insert songs) in an episode, often to highlight particularly important scenes.


Anime is different to Western cartoons as they include emotional content. Once the expectation that the aspects of visual intrigue or animation being just for children is put aside, the audience can realize that many emotions such as suffering, death, pain, struggle, and joy can all be storytelling elements utilized in anime as much as other types of media.
